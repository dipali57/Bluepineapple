Camelids

The four llamas and two camels are camelids Camelids tend to be large and are strictly herbivorous. Camelids differ from ruminants in several ways.
They have a three-chambered rather than a four-chabered digestive tract;an upper lip that is split in two with each part separetly mobile; and an
isolated incisor in the upper jaw. They have long legs that,because they lack tensor skin to bridge between thigh and body, look longer still.
